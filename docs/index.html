Spatial Data Management: A tutorial

03 Oct 2017, 11:46

Spatial data Spatial data is information about the locations and shapes of geographic features and the relationships between them, usually stored as coordinates and topology (ESRI, 2017). In other words, it is data that is connected to a ‘location’ on earth, usually represented as latitude and longitude coordinates. Such data is generated by sensors, GPS devices, smartphones and is at the center of GIS systems. Since spatial data is multidimensional (the queries involve both latitude and longitude, in some cases even time), managing it requires special techniques and indexes, some of which will be covered in the papers we read in the course.
